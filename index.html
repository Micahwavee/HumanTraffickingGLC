What is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is the illegal trade of human beings, typically for the purposes of forced labor, sexual exploitation, or slavery. Victims are often deceived, coerced, or taken against their will. This crime affects millions of people around the world regardless of age, gender, or background.

Key Statistics

  • Over 27 million people are estimated to be victims of human trafficking globally (2024).
  • 71% of trafficking victims are women and girls.
  • 1 in 4 victims of modern slavery are children.
  • Human trafficking generates over $150 billion USD annually in illicit profits.

Signs of Human Trafficking

  • Appearing fearful or anxious, especially around authority
  • Inability to speak freely or alone
  • Lack of control over identification or documents
  • Unpaid or underpaid labor
